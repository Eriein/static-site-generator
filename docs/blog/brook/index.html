Who is Brook?

Brook is the musician of the Straw Hat Pirates — a living skeleton with one of the saddest pasts on the crew, who still greets the world with a smile and a terrible joke. He ate the Revive-Revive Fruit, which brought him back to life once after he died. His soul wandered through thick fog before it found its way back to his body, and by the time it did, only bones were left. He then drifted entirely alone aboard a ghost ship for some fifty years before the Straw Hats found him.

He survived all of that holding onto a single promise: to return to Laboon, the whale his old crew left waiting at the entrance to the Grand Line.

Fun Facts

  • He ate the Yomi Yomi no Mi (Revive-Revive Fruit), which let his soul return to his body after death.
  • When his soul finally found its way back, it recognized his decayed body only by the distinctive afro.
  • He was the musician of the Rumbar Pirates long before joining the Straw Hats.
  • During the timeskip he became a worldwide music sensation under the stage name "Soul King."
  • He's 90 years old and stands well over eight feet tall.

Best Moments

  • Learning Laboon still waits — finding out the whale his old crew promised to return to is alive and well at the Twin Capes, decades later, and that he can still keep his promise.
  • "Binks' Sake" at Thriller Bark — the whole crew joining in on the song that's haunted him for half a century.
  • Brook vs. Big Mom — slipping into an Emperor's stronghold to steal a Poneglyph rubbing, getting cut to pieces, and walking out anyway.
  • The Soul King concert — turning an entire island into adoring fans while his crew runs for their lives.

Final Thoughts

Brook has lost everything twice — his crew, his body, and most of a century — and both times he came back with a song and a bad joke. He's living proof that joy isn't the absence of grief, but the choice to keep playing through it. Yohohoho.
