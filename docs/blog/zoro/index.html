Who is Zoro?

Roronoa Zoro is the Straw Hats' combatant and de facto first mate, and one of the most single-minded characters in the series. He fights with three swords — two in hand, one in his mouth — in a style he calls Santoryu, and he trains hard enough that he'll fall asleep mid-meal to do it. Everything he does traces back to a promise he made as a child to become the world's greatest swordsman.

Before joining Luffy he made his living as a bounty hunter. He was the first crewmate Luffy recruited, and he's been the crew's anchor in a fight ever since.

Fun Facts

  • His current swords are Wado Ichimonji, Sandai Kitetsu, and Enma — the last inherited from Wano's legendary Kozuki Oden.
  • His lifelong promise was made to Kuina, his childhood dojo rival, who died in an accident before they could settle their score.
  • He has a famously catastrophic sense of direction and can get lost walking in a straight line.
  • Oda has said he based Zoro partly on the real-life pirate François l'Olonnais.
  • His bounty after Wano is 1,111,000,000 berries.

Best Moments

  • "Nothing happened" — at Thriller Bark, after taking on all of Luffy's accumulated pain from Kuma. Sanji finds him standing in his own blood, and Zoro tells him nothing happened.
  • The Mihawk duel at the Baratie — Zoro seeks out the world's strongest swordsman, loses badly, and asks to be cut across the chest rather than the back so his defeat won't be a wound in his back.
  • Asura vs. Kaku — the technique that turns his three swords into the illusion of nine.
  • The King fight in Wano — his sword Enma pushes him past his own limits and into a new tier of Haki entirely.

Final Thoughts

Zoro is the clearest proof that One Piece's supporting cast carries as much weight as its lead. He has his own dream, his own code, and his own reasons, and he shoulders all of it without ever asking anyone to notice.
